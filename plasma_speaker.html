Plasma Speaker

A loudspeaker which varies air pressure via a high-energy electrical plasma. Just plug in your phone, turn on some good tunes and voila! Music!

Description

The plasma speaker was something I designed for the SPIE club at University of Alberta. I wanted to use it as a tool to help inspire other engineers that they could build cool projects too. The club focused on funding its members who wanted to build there own projects. This gave people the opportunity to build what every they wanted, weather they had a team or just wanted to work on something individually. The plasma speaker was created to show others that building cool projects like this is achievable and the club could give you the resources they need. In addition to club promotion, I thought it would be a great project to help advance my analog circuit design knowledge. It proved to be a very challenging task and took me quite some time to get right.

How it works

I have made a couple versions of plasma speakers but they both use very similar methods. They both require to modulate the audio signal using pulse width modulation. Then take the new signal and amplify it using a class D amplifier circuit. Then feed the amplified signal into low pass filter to demodulate the signal. This new signal is similar to the original signal (well close to it, unfortunately a lot of noise was introduced in my design) but is stronger. The inductor of the low pass filter is a fly back tranformer. The tranformer converters the high current signal to a high voltage signal. The breakdown voltage of air is 3 kV/mm so this high voltage is required inorder to form the plamsa arc. The best way I found to conceptualize what is happening is to imagine the plasma arc as a cylinder in space. The plasma cylinder increases and decreases its diameter depending on the voltage across the arc. If we change the diameter in a similar way to the way we move a speaker back and forth we can create the same vibrations in the air, therefore creating sound. Its a pretty amazing and creative way to make sound.
